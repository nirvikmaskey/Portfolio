Melons and Cherries

extractive realities and ecological resonances

SCROLL

DOCUMENTATION

In the first two weeks of July 2024, we explored Athens' urban, suburban, and rural landscapes through the lens of critical ecologies. Weaving connections between wildfires, wildlfe, tourism, gentrification, pollution, agriculture, immigration and economy.

We passed through cement industries and shipwrecks, oil refineries and oil spills, farming lands and global logistics warehouses.


Our process involved tracing "the sacred way" of the Eleusinian Mysteries and engaging in conversations with people we met along the way.(Click here the sacred way on RouteYou).


Our responses took the shape of a performance, sharing melons and cherries on a table covered by two paper tablecloth with a layer of oil collected from the oil spill underneath. It was accompanied by images made with materials we found along the way. It was an attempt to create a space where we could share our reflections and stories in a sensitive way. We exhibited our outcomes as a part of the Exhibition "Common Grounds I&II" at SIC in Athens and at the Clock Tower of Aspropyrgos.

PROCCESS



A group of four, Hal, HaiRong, Lucy and me decided to embark on a 27.5 Km walk tracing the sacred way of the Eleusinian Mysteries. We began our walk at 5 A.M. from Athens towards Elefsina. The landscapes around us transitioned from city to suburbs to forests and hills. We encountered cicadas, turtles, a patch of burnt forest, a shooting range and very kind people along the way.


While the morning started out very well, we were under prepared for the harsh 38C sun and difficult terrain. We came across a small family run shop in a small settlement after we crossed Mount Aigaleo. There we met up with prof. Imamura and took our first big break at the shop where he waited for us . When Yannis and Chirstiana from the shop understood that we walked all the way there from Athens and that we were attempting to walk the entierity of the sacred road, they offered us free water, melons and cherries. They were the most delicious melons and cherries we had. After a few hours of conversing through google translate, we continued our journey.

While we had to call an end to our walk due to symptoms of heatstroke and a section next to highway traffic, this experience greatly shaped our final responses at the exhibition "Common Grounds"
